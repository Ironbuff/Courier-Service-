An import service in the context of logistics, such as courier or shipping, refers to the process of transporting goods, packages, or documents from one country to another. These services handle various stages of the shipment journey, including collection, transportation, customs clearance, and final delivery. Import services cater to businesses and individuals, ensuring that international shipments comply with legal and regulatory requirements.

An export service facilitates the shipment of goods, packages, or documents from one country to another. It includes processes like pickup, transportation, customs clearance, and delivery at the destination. Export services assist with compliance to international trade regulations, documentation, and often offer features like tracking and insurance, ensuring secure and timely delivery of goods across borders.

Free delivery is a service where the seller covers the shipping costs, providing customers with no additional charges for the transportation of goods. It enhances customer satisfaction, encourages purchases, and is often used as a promotional strategy by businesses.
